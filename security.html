Security Classifications

Veiled Phenomena are classified to indicate threat level and containment difficulty. These classifications guide the Directiveâ€™s response strategies.

Class Description Containment Protocol
Theta Harmless or well-understood phenomena with no threat to public safety. Standard monitoring and documentation.
Omicron Potentially dangerous but stable; requires routine containment. Regular containment measures with limited public exposure.
Delta Active threat to public safety, unpredictable behavior. Strict containment with Null Response Team involvement.
Epsilon Uncontainable, reality-altering, or godlike phenomena. Extreme protocols; research prioritized over containment.

Additional Notes

Classifications may change as research evolves or incidents occur. Some phenomena defy clear categorization and require special task forces.
